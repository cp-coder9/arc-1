# Architex CPD Professional Body Research Matrix

Purpose: prevent Architex from treating all CPD as one generic credit system. Each learner/project role must resolve to the correct professional body, registration category, CPD unit, cycle, category structure and evidence workflow.

## Architecture — SACAP

CPD required: yes, for registered architectural professionals.

Workflow implications:
- Use SACAP Category 1 / 2 / 3 structure.
- Category 1 developmental activities are the strategic CPD-access target.
- 10 hours = 1 credit; 1 hour = 0.1 credit for Category 1 developmental activities.
- Minimum Category 1: 1 credit per year and 5 over 5-year cycle.
- Architex must not self-declare Category 1; CPD Central, a recognised VA, professional body or accreditor must approve category and credit value.

Role routing:
- Architectural professional → SACAP.

## Engineering — ECSA

CPD required: yes, for professional/specified engineering registration categories. Candidate categories are excluded from the renewal CPD requirement but may still need development toward registration.

Core structure:
- 5-year cycle.
- 25 credits over 5 years.
- 1 credit = 10 notional hours.
- At least 5 credits per cycle must be from Category 1a validated educational/developmental activities.
- Minimum 3 credits per annum across at least two of the three categories.
- No carry-over into the next cycle.

Categories:
- Category 1: Developmental Activities.
  - 1a validated educational/developmental activities: conferences, congresses, workshops, lectures, seminars, courses, colloquiums, HEI study.
  - 1b tertiary studies.
- Category 2: Work-Based Activities.
  - Engineering work, mentoring candidates, in-house skills development.
- Category 3: Individual Activities.
  - VA membership, engineering community contributions, publications, presentations, committee/task group work, course development.

Workflow implications:
- Structural/civil/electrical/mechanical engineers must route to ECSA rules.
- For Architex courses, the high-value target is ECSA Category 1a validation.
- Certificates must store validating body / licensed body / verified provider reference.
- Do not issue ECSA wording unless validation is confirmed.

Role routing:
- Structural engineer → ECSA.
- Civil engineer → ECSA.
- Electrical engineer → ECSA.
- Mechanical engineer → ECSA.

## Quantity Surveying — SACQSP

CPD required: yes, for professional quantity surveyors. Candidates are generally exempt for first 5 years unless APC requirements are not completed.

Core structure:
- SACQSP uses CPD hours, not the same “credits” model as SACAP/ECSA.
- 125 hours over 5 years.
- 25 hours per annum.
- Category 1 minimum: 10 hours/year.
- Category 2 maximum: 15 hours/year.
- Annual cycle: 1 January to 31 December.
- Records due within 30 days after year end.
- Annual Code of Professional Conduct online assessment has a 72% pass mark.

Categories:
- Category 1: structured/validated CPD such as conferences, congresses, formal workshops, lectures, seminars, distance-learning seminars, formal tertiary studies, peer-reviewed publications and accredited presentations.
- Category 2: other professional development such as in-house skills training, small-group discussions, professional administration with built-environment presentations, non-formally evaluated self-study, teaching and postgraduate supervision/evaluation.

Workflow implications:
- Quantity surveyor users must see hours, not credits/points, unless a specific body-approved activity says otherwise.
- The certificate/evidence workflow must track Category 1 vs Category 2 hours and the annual 25-hour target.
- CPD assessments can be valuable for Category 1 evidence only when validated/approved by the appropriate SACQSP/VA workflow.

Role routing:
- Quantity surveyor → SACQSP.

## Construction Project Management / Construction Management — SACPCMP

CPD required: yes, for registered persons covered by SACPCMP policy. Candidates are excluded under the cited policy.

Core structure:
- Current cited Board Notice 381 of 2022 runs 1 April 2023 to 31 March 2028.
- Cycle: 3 financial years.
- Total: 300 CPD credits.

Categories and cycle totals:
- Professional Practice: 100 credits.
- Personal Development: 50 credits.
- Mentorship: 100 credits.
- PPPI: 50 credits.

Yearly target pattern:
- Year 1: 50 / 25 / 50 / 25.
- Year 2: 30 / 15 / 30 / 15.
- Year 3: 20 / 10 / 20 / 10.

Workflow implications:
- Project manager/construction manager workflows must not use SACAP/ECSA 0.1-credit/hour assumptions.
- Architex must show category-specific SACPCMP targets and year-of-cycle status.
- Assessment/course values should be partner-approved or manually verified against SACPCMP activity/provider approval.

Role routing:
- Construction project manager → SACPCMP.
- Construction manager → SACPCMP.

## Planning — SACPLAN

CPD required: yes.

Core structure:
- Points model, not SACAP credits.
- 75 points over a 3-year cycle.
- Categories include:
  - Category A: Professional Knowledge.
  - Category B: Mentorship.
  - Category C: Active Participation in the Profession.
- Some planning-related non-credit-bearing courses are 1 point per hour, subject to focus-area rules and limits.

Workflow implications:
- Professional planner users must see points, not architectural credits.
- Course/assessment values must be stored as SACPLAN points.

Role routing:
- Professional planner → SACPLAN.

## Landscape Architecture — SACLAP

CPD required: yes, for landscape architectural professionals.

Core structure:
- 5-year cycle beginning 1 April after registration.
- Annual submission due end March.
- Full professionals, senior technologists and technologists: 25 credits over 5 years.
- Technicians and technical assistants: 20 credits over 5 years.
- Minimum 3 prerequisite credits per annum.
- Minimum 1 Category 1 credit per annum.

Categories:
- Category 1: Personal Professional Development.
  - Seminars, workshops, conferences, colloquiums, educational short courses and e-based activities.
  - 1 hour = 0.1 credits; 10 hours = 1 credit.
- Category 2: Further Studies.
- Category 3: Research and Publications.
- Category 4: Teaching and Training.
- Category 5: Professional Practice.
- Category 6: Professional and Community Engagement.

Workflow implications:
- Landscape professionals must use SACLAP Category 1-6 structure, not SACAP architecture rules.
- The main Architex assessment target is SACLAP Category 1 personal professional development, subject to VA/SACLAP accreditation/endorsement.
- Role category matters because technicians/technical assistants have a 20-credit cycle target while full professional/technologist categories have 25.

Role routing:
- Landscape architectural professional → SACLAP.

## Geomatics — SAGC

CPD required: yes, for professional, technologist and technician geomatics categories. Candidate and certain exempt/non-practising categories are treated differently.

Core structure:
- 5-year cycle starting 1 June after registration.
- Professionals/technologists: 20 credits over 5 years.
- Technicians: 13 credits over 5 years.
- Professionals/technologists: at least 3 credits per annum in at least two categories.
- Technicians: at least 2 credits per annum.
- At least 5 credits per cycle from Category 1.
- At least 1 ethics-related credit per 5-year cycle.

Categories:
- Category 1: Developmental Activities.
  - Conferences, seminars, courses, refresher courses and approved educational/vendor training.
  - 10 hours = 1 credit; half-day = 0.5 credit, full day = 1 credit.
  - Combined annual max Category 1: 5 credits.
- Category 2: Work-Based Activities.
  - Mentoring candidates: 40 contact hours = 1 credit, max 2/year.
  - In-house lecturing/skills training: 10 hours = 1 credit, max 2/year.
  - R&D/technology/knowledge sharing.
- Category 3: Individual Activities.
  - Recognised VA membership, lecturing, moderator/external examiner work and other professional activities.

Workflow implications:
- Geomatics users need professional/technologist/technician distinction because the cycle target differs.
- The assessment platform must track ethics-credit status.
- Approved provider evidence is important: recognised VAs and accredited educational institutions are approved for Category 1 unless SAGC specifies otherwise.

Role routing:
- Geomatics practitioner → SAGC.

## Property Valuation — SACPVP

CPD required: yes, based on official SACPVP notices/pages confirming CPD compliance affects certificate validity.

Research status:
- Official information found confirms CPD compliance is mandatory for Professional Valuers, Professional Associated Valuers and Single Residential Property Assessors.
- Compliant practitioners may receive 5-year certificate validity; non-compliant may receive 1-year validity.
- Detailed current points/hours/category formula was not found in the official public material reviewed.

Workflow implications:
- Do not guess SACPVP points, hours or categories.
- Architex should create a compliance-status workflow only until official rules or a partner confirmation is provided.
- Store certificate validity, SACPVP compliance evidence and manual review state.

Role routing:
- Property valuer → SACPVP.

## Product structure required from this research

1. User onboarding must ask for role and registration body, not just “professional”.
2. Role selection must default the CPD rule engine:
   - Architect → SACAP.
   - Structural engineer → ECSA.
   - Quantity surveyor → SACQSP.
   - Construction project manager → SACPCMP.
   - Planner → SACPLAN.
   - Landscape architect → SACLAP.
   - Geomatics practitioner → SAGC.
   - Property valuer → SACPVP manual-compliance mode.
3. The learner dashboard must show the correct unit label: credits, points or hours.
4. Assessment certificates must use the professional-body-specific category wording.
5. Accreditation admin must prevent publishing CPD claims unless body/category/value/provider evidence is complete.
6. Where rules are preliminary or not found, the system must degrade to manual verification rather than inventing values.
